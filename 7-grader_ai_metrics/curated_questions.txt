#1
Q: "I control [[Vizier of Remedies]] and [[Devoted Druid]]. Can I generate infinite Green mana?"
A: "Yes, you can. This works because the Vizier creates a replacement effect that modifies the cost of the Druid's untap ability. When you activate the Druid, the cost requires putting a -1/-1 counter on it, but the Vizier reduces that number by one, meaning you effectively pay 0 counters. Since the Druid never actually receives the counter, it doesn't shrink or die, allowing you to untap it infinitely."
difficulty: "hard"

#2
Q: "I attack with [[Questing Beast]] and my opponent casts [[Fog]]. Does damage go through?"
A: "Yes, with Questing Beast attacking, casting Fog prevents damage to your opponent's creatures and planeswalkers, but NOT the combat damage Questing Beast deals to the opponent, as its ability overrides prevention effects for damage dealt by it. Questing Beast's 'combat damage can't be prevented' text stops Fog from stopping the player damage."
difficulty: "hard"

#3
Q: "If my opponent casts [[Solitude]] on my [[Ketramose]] during my turn, do I draw a card from [[Ketramose]]'s ability?"
A: "Yes, you will draw a card. Ketramose's ability is a triggered ability that looks back at the game state to see if its trigger condition was met, and it functions even if Ketramose itself leaves the battlefield as part of the event or before the ability resolves."
difficulty: "hard"

#4
Q: "I cast [[Dress Down]] and my opponent has a [[Magus of the Moon]] in play. Are my non-basic lands still mountains?"
A: "Yes, your non-basic lands will still be mountains even after casting Dress Down on Magus of the Moon, because Magus's effect (changing land types in Layer 4) applies before Dress Down's effect (removing abilities in Layer 6), meaning the lands become mountains before the Magus loses its ability to do so."
difficulty: "hard"

A: "Yes, the lands remain Mountains. This happens because type-changing effects are applied in Layer 4, whereas ability-removing effects occur later in Layer 6. Since the game applies the "Mountain" type before removing the Dryad's text, Valakut triggers will still function normally. However, the opponent does lose the specific permission to play an additional land for that turn."
difficulty: "hard"

It loses its lore counters and won't gain more, but its existing chapter abilities become usable on the Mountain."

#7

A: "Yes, the Dragonhawk's end-step ability will still trigger and deal 4 damage to your opponent, even though it died, because the ability creates a delayed trigger that exists independently on the stack, separate from its source. The key is that the ability triggered and put the cards into exile and the damage effect on the stack; as long as the cards are still in exile, the delayed trigger resolves and deals damage based on those specific exiled cards."

A: "Your opponent draws 2 cards. This happens because activated abilities exist independently of their source once placed on the stack; removing The One Ring does not counter the ability. Although a new burden counter cannot be added to the now-exiled card, the game uses Last Known Information to recall the two counters present when it left the battlefield, allowing the draw effect to resolve based on that count."

#18
Q: "If I cast [[Treachery]] on an opponent's [[Eidolon of Blossoms]], do I draw a card? If so, what is the rule?
A: Yes. You control the Eidolon as soon as Treachery enters. Then the Eidolon sees that another enchantment you control entered, and triggers. You'll draw a card. This is defined by Rule 611.3c, which establishes that continuous effects generated by a permanent apply simultaneously with its entry into the battlefield. Because the control change happens at the exact same moment Treachery lands, the game state sees you as the controller of the Eidolon in time for it to see the enchantment enter."
difficulty: "hard"

Although the Master of Waves leaves play briefly (removing the +1/+1 bonus), he returns immediately. By the time the spell finishes resolving and the game actually checks the board state, the Master is back and the tokens are safe with the bonus reapplied."

Clone copies what's printed on the card, and Lignify's continuous effect is applied after the copy is made, so your Clone will be a copy of the base creature with its own abilities, not a Treefolk."
